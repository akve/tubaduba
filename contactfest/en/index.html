Intensives highlight



Anya Cloud
Contact as an Act of Resistance

This CI intensive will focus on developing an open system Contact Body that increases possibilities and cultivates radical aliveness. We will experiment with a multiplicity of ways to take our humanness in and out of physical contact

We will experiment with a multiplicity of ways to take our humanness in and out of physical contact and notice what that does to our evolving definition/experience of Contact Improvisation. We will practice endurance, permeability, durability, vulnerability, nuance, and intelligence. Through highlighting and accessing specificity within physical and energetic spatial pathways we will increase our aesthetic and sensorial range. We will question, provoke, and tangle with aspects of identity, power, expectations, composition, and communication. We will work to find agency in everything we do. We will set boundaries, take risks, and sweat. The politics of responsive resistance will offer a framework for inquiry, transgression, and generosity within our dancing. It is a high stakes and high physicality proposition. Some of the research will be quite practical and some will be quite elusive. We will consider why and how our dancing is relevant in the current world. It is an ‘all in all the time’ intensive that is designed for experienced dancers.

Melanie Sorin
Morning Gaga intensive

The Gaga movement language, developed by Batsheva Dance Company's artistic director Ohad Naharin, originated from the belief in the healing, dynamic, ever-changing power of movement. The method offers a framework for discovering

and strengthening the body, for challenging our movement habits and exploring the endless possibilities in a gentle way, while lightening our senses and imagination. Gaga offers an experience of freedom and pleasure, each one with himself and with others. "We connect to the sense of the endlessness of possibilities. Yielding is constant while we are ready to snap... We explore multi-dimensional movement, we enjoy the burning sensation in our muscles, we are aware of our explosive power and sometimes we use it. We change our movement habits by finding new ones, we can be calm and alert at once. We become available . . .” - Ohad Naharin

Ruslan Baranov (Santah)
& Marina Baranova

Poetry of simple things.
Once, I read a phrase by Danny Lepkoff: "Dancing CI in a simple way - that's a talent". And these words still inspire me.

Once, pretty long time ago, I read a phrase by Danny Lepkoff: "Dancing CI in a simple way - that's a talent". And these words still inspire me. To see the beauty in simple things, do not over-complicate, easily accept and let go, feel satisfied with small, but aim for bigger, find interest in what's happening, enjoy the details, find the comfort zone. And enjoy simple moments in our dances. That's what we'd love to focus on our classes.

Yochai Ginton & Olivia Court Mesa
The Common Body

it's a workshop-laboratory and movement language that is aiming to enrich within dancers the experience in dancing with a partner.

Olivia & Yochai are exploring and mixing contact improvisation, dynamic floor work and contemporary partnering, finding a unique vocabulary between real time communication and form. Their unique approach triggers the dancer/mover’s mind to become sharper yet playful and curious. Releasing a wide spectrum of acrobatic possibilities in his own body/movement and with a partner.

Kids and Adults intensive
"Learning the world through playing"

Sergey Golovnea, Sasha Soshnikova, Stan Potoku, Olya Braga.
How amazing! There will be a time and place to dance all together, kids and parents!

Together with other kids, together with other adults.
Dance! Sing! Share! Create seen and invisible contacts :))
 
WE PROPOSE TO SPEND TIME TOGETHER, IN FREE MOVEMENT, SOUND AND PLAY.

The most valuable (and priceless) - is to feel the kids' trust, mostly not to the parents but to themselves: to their source of art, to their identity, to their possibilities of body, voice, imagination.
The same applies to us, adults.
Everyone of us can be a guide to someone on this playful journey!
Through imagery and games, we'll be travelling to different worlds. We'll be going deep, climb mountains, fly, safely fall, challenge obstacles, build cities, meet unknown creatures, share the experiences of trust, flexibility, courage, curiosity, humor, attention, listening, flow...
Possibly, we'll create new games and new Universes built on our imagination
To support the art space:
- Dance as a play, as a meeting, as communication, as meeting point of two creative sources. 
– Singing. Free sound, rhytmic games 
- Curiosity, trust, play and improvisation

P.S. This space is for kids and parents of any age
